The Soil

Our roots reach deep into the limestone-rich earth of the valley, where every mineral tells a story of prehistoric oceans.

The Patience

We wait for the grape to reach its zenith. There is no clock in the cellar, only the slow heartbeat of fermentation.

The Craft

Passed down through five generations, our methods remain stubbornly manual, honoring the touch of the artisan.

A Century of Craftsmanship

1894

The Founding Father

Arthur Montague acquired the first ten acres of the Cellar estate. Armed with nothing but a surveyor’s map and a passion for French oak, he planted the original 'Old Block' vines that still produce our flagship reserve today.

1923

The Secret Vintages

During the years of prohibition, the cellar remained active in shadow. These 'lost' vintages were preserved in a hidden subterranean chamber discovered only in 1982.

1958

The Modern Renaissance

Introduction of temperature-controlled fermentation revolutionized our consistency while maintaining artisanal flavor profiles.

2004

Sustainability & Soil

Under the leadership of Elena Montague, the estate transitioned to fully organic viticulture. We introduced biodiversity corridors, allowing the natural flora of the valley to protect our vines.
